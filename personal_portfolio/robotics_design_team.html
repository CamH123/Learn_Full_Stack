Rice Robotics Team - Lunar Rover

Background:

Sponsored by the Unmanned Exploration Laboratory, we are developing a cutting-edge machine learning algorithm and sample collection device to enable our rovers to autonomously navigate and analyze the lunar surface, specifically searching for water in lunar lava tubes. This project leverages advanced skills in computer science, electronics, and mechanical engineering to contribute to lunar habitation initiatives.

Lunar Rover

Background:

Using ROS 2 and Python, we developed the movement, sensing, and navigation capabilities of the lunar rover. ROS 2 provided a modular framework to integrate various components, such as motor controllers, sensors, and cameras, allowing seamless communication between them. Python scripts were used to implement algorithms for tasks like obstacle avoidance, environment mapping, and path planning, enabling the rover to autonomously explore and navigate the lunar surface with precision.
