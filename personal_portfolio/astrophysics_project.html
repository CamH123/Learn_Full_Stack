Near-Earth Asteroid orbit determination and Simulator

Background:

Asteroid Studied: 1998 RO4

Results

Telescopic images from three observation sessions were used to estimate the orbital elements and absolute H magnitude of near-Earth asteroid 1998 RO4. The method of Gauss and a Monte Carlo simulation were used to determine that 1998 RO4 has a semi-major axis of 2.130±0.009 AU, an eccentricity of 0.423±0.002, an inclination of 5.36±0.01°, a longitude of the ascending node of 191.77±0.08°, an argument of periapsis of 111.95±0.10°, and a mean anomaly on 2023 June 27 6:26:2.155 of 352.09±0.06°. Linear regression was used to ascertain that 1998 RO4 has an absolute H magnitude of 18.6. The Rebound simulation using these orbital elements showed that over 50 million years, 25.00% remained in orbit, 13.33% crashed into the sun, and 61.67% were ejected from the solar system, showing that it is improbable that 1998 RO4 will collide with Earth within this timeframe.
